    74) ! Here is some information about how the incremental remapping scheme
    75) ! works in CICE and how it can be adapted for use in other models.  
    76) !
    77) ! The remapping routine is designed to transport a generic mass-like 
    78) ! field (in CICE, the ice fractional area) along with an arbitrary number
    79) ! of tracers in two dimensions.  The velocity components are assumed 
    80) ! to lie at grid cell corners and the transported scalars at cell centers. 
    81) ! Incremental remapping has the following desirable properties: 
    82) ! 
    83) ! (1) Tracer monotonicity is preserved.  That is, no new local 
    84) !     extrema are produced in fields like ice thickness or internal 
    85) !     energy. 
    86) ! (2) The reconstucted mass and tracer fields vary linearly in x and y. 
    87) !     This means that remapping is 2nd-order accurate in space, 
    88) !     except where horizontal gradients are limited to preserve 
    89) !     monotonicity. 
    90) ! (3) There are economies of scale.  Transporting a single field 
    91) !     is rather expensive, but additional fields have a relatively 
    92) !     low marginal cost. 
    93) ! 
    94) ! The following generic conservation equations may be solved: 
    95) ! 
    96) !            dm/dt = del*(u*m)             (0) 
    97) !       d(m*T1)/dt = del*(u*m*T1)          (1) 
    98) !    d(m*T1*T2)/dt = del*(u*m*T1*T2)       (2) 
    99) ! d(m*T1*T2*T3)/dt = del*(u*m*T1*T2*T3)    (3) 
   100) !
   101) ! where d is a partial derivative, del is the 2D divergence operator,
   102) ! u is the horizontal velocity, m is the mass density field, and
   103) ! T1, T2, and T3 are tracers.
   104) !
   105) ! In CICE, these equations have the form
   106) ! 
   107) !               da/dt = del*(u*a)          (4)
   108) ! dv/dt =   d(a*h)/dt = del*(u*a*h)        (5)
   109) ! de/dt = d(a*h*q)/dt = del*(u*a*h*q)      (6)
   110) !            d(aT)/dt = del*(u*a*t)        (7)
   111) ! 
   112) ! where a = fractional ice area, v = ice/snow volume, h = v/a = thickness, 
   113) ! e = ice/snow internal energy (J/m^2), q = e/v = internal energy per 
   114) ! unit volume (J/m^3), and T is a tracer.  These equations express 
   115) ! conservation of ice area, volume, internal energy, and area-weighted
   116) ! tracer, respectively. 
   117) !
   118) ! (Note: In CICE, a, v and e are prognostic quantities from which
   119) !  h and q are diagnosed.  The remapping routine works with tracers,
   120) !  which means that h and q must be derived from a, v, and e before
   121) !  calling the remapping routine.)  
   122) !
   123) ! Earlier versions of CICE assumed fixed ice and snow density. 
   124) ! Beginning with CICE 4.0, the ice and snow density can be variable. 
   125) ! In this case, equations (5) and (6) are replaced by 
   126) ! 
   127) ! dv/dt =        d(a*h)/dt = del*(u*a*h)          (8)  
   128) ! dm/dt =    d(a*h*rho)/dt = del*(u*a*h*rho)      (9)
   129) ! de/dt = d(a*h*rho*qm)/dt = del*(u*a*h*rho*qm)   (10)
   130) ! 
   131) ! where rho = density and qm = internal energy per unit mass (J/kg). 
   132) ! Eq. (9) expresses mass conservation, which in the variable-density 
   133) ! case is no longer equivalent to volume conservation (8). 
   134) !
   135) ! Tracers satisfying equations of the form (1) are called "type 1." 
   136) ! In CICE the paradigmatic type 1 tracers are hi and hs. 
   137) ! 
   138) ! Tracers satisfying equations of the form (2) are called "type 2". 
   139) ! The paradigmatic type 2 tracers are qi and qs (or rhoi and rhos 
   140) !  in the variable-density case). 
   141) ! 
   142) ! Tracers satisfying equations of the form (3) are called "type 3."
   143) ! The paradigmatic type 3 tracers are qmi and qms in the variable-density
   144) ! case.  There are no such tracers in the constant-density case. 
   145) ! 
   146) ! The fields a, T1, and T2 are reconstructed in each grid cell with 
   147) ! 2nd-order accuracy.  T3 is reconstructed with 1st-order accuracy 
   148) ! (i.e., it is transported in upwind fashion) in order to avoid 
   149) ! additional mathematical complexity. 
   150) ! 
   151) ! The mass-like field lives in the array "mm" (shorthand for mean 
   152) ! mass) and the tracers fields in the array "tm" (mean tracers). 
   153) ! In order to transport tracers correctly, the remapping routine 
   154) ! needs to know the tracers types and relationships.  This is done 
   155) ! as follows: 
   156) ! 
   157) ! Each field in the "tm" array is assigned an index, 1:max_ntrace. 
   158) ! (Note: max_ntrace is not the same as max_ntrcr, the number of tracers 
   159) ! in the trcrn state variable array.  For remapping purposes we 
   160) ! have additional tracers hi, hs, qi and qs.) 
   161) ! For standard CICE with ntrcr = 1, nilyr = 4, and nslyr = 1, the 
   162) ! indexing is as follows: 
   163) ! 1   = hi 
   164) ! 2   = hs 
   165) ! 3   = Ts 
   166) ! 4-7 = qi 
   167) ! 8   = qs 
   168) ! 
   169) ! The tracer types (1,2,3) are contained in the "tracer_type" array. 
   170) ! For standard CICE: 
   171) ! 
   172) !     tracer_type = (1 1 1 2 2 2 2 2) 
   173) ! 
   174) ! Type 2 and type 3 tracers are said to depend on type 1 tracers. 
   175) ! For instance, qi depends on hi, which is to say that 
   176) ! there is a conservation equation of the form (2) or (6). 
   177) ! Thus we define a "depend" array.  For standard CICE: 
   178) ! 
   179) !          depend = (0 0 0 1 1 1 1 2) 
   180) ! 
   181) ! which implies that elements 1-3 (hi, hs, Ts) are type 1, 
   182) ! elements 4-7 (qi) depend on element 1 (hi), and element 8 (qs) 
   183) ! depends on element 2 (hs). 
   184) !
   185) ! We also define a logical array "has_dependents".  In standard CICE: 
   186) ! 
   187) !  has_dependents = (T T F F F F F F), 
   188) ! 
   189) ! which means that only elements 1 and 2 (hi and hs) have dependent 
   190) ! tracers. 
   191) ! 
   192) ! For the variable-density case, things are a bit more complicated. 
   193) ! Suppose we have 4 variable-density ice layers and one variable- 
   194) ! density snow layer.  Then the indexing is as follows: 
   195) ! 1    = hi 
   196) ! 2    = hs 
   197) ! 3    = Ts 
   198) ! 4-7  = rhoi 
   199) ! 8    = rhos 
   200) ! 9-12 = qmi 
   201) ! 13   = qms 
   202) ! 
   203) ! The key arrays are: 
   204) ! 
   205) !    tracer_type = (1 1 1 2 2 2 2 2 3 3 3 3 3) 
   206) ! 
   207) !         depend = (0 0 0 1 1 1 1 2 4 5 6 7 8) 
   208) ! 
   209) ! has_dependents = (T T F T T T T T F F F F F) 
   210) ! 
   211) ! which imply that hi and hs are type 1 with dependents rhoi and rhos, 
   212) ! while rhoi and rhos are type 2 with dependents qmi and qms. 
   213) ! 
   214) ! Tracers added to the ntrcr array are handled automatically 
   215) ! by the remapping with little extra coding.  It is necessary 
   216) ! only to provide the correct type and dependency information. 
   217) !
   218) ! When using this routine in other models, most of the tracer dependency
   219) ! apparatus may be irrelevant.  In a layered ocean model, for example,
   220) ! the transported fields are the layer thickness h (the mass density
   221) ! field) and two or more tracers (T, S, and various trace species).
   222) ! Suppose there are just two tracers, T and S.  Then the tracer arrays
   223) ! have the values:
   224) !
   225) !    tracer_type = (1 1)
   226) !         depend = (0 0)
   227) ! has_dependents = (F F)
   228) !
   229) ! which is to say that all tracer transport equations are of the form (1).
   230) !
   231) ! The tracer dependency arrays are optional input arguments for the
   232) ! main remapping subroutine.  If these arrays are not passed in, they
   233) ! take on the default values tracer_type(:) = 1, depend(:) = 0, and
   234) ! has_dependents(:) = F, which are appropriate for most purposes.
   235) !
   236) ! Another optional argument is integral_order.  If integral_order = 1,
   237) ! then the triangle integrals are exact for linear functions of x and y.
   238) ! If integral_order = 2, these integrals are exact for both linear and
   239) ! quadratic functions.  If integral_order = 3, integrals are exact for
   240) ! cubic functions as well.  If all tracers are of type 1, then the
   241) ! integrals of mass*tracer are quadratic, and integral_order = 2 is
   242) ! sufficient.  In CICE, where there are type 2 tracers, we integrate
   243) ! functions of the form mass*tracer1*tracer2.  Thus integral_order = 3
   244) ! is required for exactness, though integral_order = 2 may be good enough
   245) ! in practice.
   246) !
   247) ! Finally, a few words about the edgearea fields:
   248) !
   249) ! In earlier versions of this scheme, the divergence of the velocity
   250) ! field implied by the remapping was, in general, different from the
   251) ! value of del*u computed in the dynamics.  For energetic consistency
   252) ! (in CICE as well as in layered ocean models such as HYPOP),
   253) ! these two values should agree.  This can be ensured by setting
   254) ! l_fixed_area = T and specifying the area transported across each grid
   255) ! cell edge in the arrays edgearea_e and edgearea_n.  The departure
   256) ! regions are then tweaked, following an idea by Mats Bentsen, such
   257) ! that they have the desired area.  If l_fixed_area = F, these regions
   258) ! are not tweaked, and the edgearea arrays are output variables.
   259) !

   316)             yyav(i,j,iblk) = c1/c12
   317)             xyav(i,j,iblk) = c0
   318)             xxxav(i,j,iblk) = c0
   319)             xxyav(i,j,iblk) = c0
   320)             xyyav(i,j,iblk) = c0
   321)             yyyav(i,j,iblk) = c0

  1051) !

  1053) !

  1055) !  in that grid cell are assumed to have no physical meaning.
  1056) !

  1058) !  (abs(tm) < puny), then the values of its dependent tracers in that
  1059) !  grid cell are assumed to have no physical meaning.
  1060) ! For example, the enthalpy value has no meaning if the thickness
  1061) !  is zero.
  1062) !
  1063) ! !REVISION HISTORY:
  1064) !
  1065) ! author William H. Lipscomb, LANL
  1066) !
  1067) ! !USES:
  1068) !
  1069) ! !INPUT/OUTPUT PARAMETERS:
  1070) !
  1071)       integer (kind=int_kind), intent(in) ::     &
  1072)            nx_block, ny_block  ,&! block dimensions
  1073)            ilo,ihi,jlo,jhi     ,&! beginning and end of physical domain
  1074)            nghost              ,&! number of ghost cells
  1075)            ntrace                ! number of tracers in use
  1076)

  1087)

  1090)            mm            ! mean ice area in each grid cell
  1091) 
  1092)       real (kind=dbl_kind), dimension (nx_block,ny_block,0:ncat),     &
  1093)            intent(out) ::     &
  1094)            mmask         ! = 1. if ice is present, else = 0.
  1095) 
  1096)       real (kind=dbl_kind), dimension (nx_block, ny_block, max_ntrace, ncat),  &
  1097)            intent(in), optional ::     &
  1098)            tm            ! mean tracer values in each grid cell
  1099) 
  1100)       real (kind=dbl_kind), dimension (nx_block, ny_block, max_ntrace, ncat),  &
  1101)            intent(out), optional ::     &
  1102)            tmask         ! = 1. if tracer is present, else = 0.
  1103) !

  1105) !

  1120)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------

  1123)       do j = 1, ny_block
  1124)       do i = 1, nx_block
  1125)          if (mm(i,j,0) > puny) then
  1126)             mmask(i,j,0) = c1

  1144)          do j = 1, ny_block
  1145)          do i = 1, nx_block
  1146)             if (mm(i,j,n) > puny) then
  1147)                icells(n) = icells(n) + 1
  1148)                ij = icells(n)
  1149)                indxi(ij,n) = i
  1150)                indxj(ij,n) = j

  1160)          do ij = 1, icells(n)

  1162)             j = indxj(ij,n)
  1163)             mmask(i,j,n) = c1
  1164)          enddo
  1165) 
  1166)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  1167)     ! tracer masks
  1168)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  1169) 
  1170)          if (present(tm)) then
  1171) 
  1172)             tmask(:,:,:,n) = c0
  1173)             do nt = 1, ntrace
  1174)                if (has_dependents(nt)) then
  1175)                   do ij = 1, icells(n)
  1176)                      i = indxi(ij,n)
  1177)                      j = indxj(ij,n)
  1178)                      if (abs(tm(i,j,nt,n)) > puny) then
  1179)                         tmask(i,j,nt,n) = c1

  1182)                endif
  1183)             enddo
  1184) 
  1185)          endif                     ! present(tm)

  1188)     ! Redefine icells

  1190)     ! For nghost = 2, include one layer of ghost cells

  1192) 
  1193)          icells(n) = 0
  1194)          do j = jlo-nghost+1, jhi+nghost-1
  1195)          do i = ilo-nghost+1, ihi+nghost-1
  1196)             if (mm(i,j,n) > puny) then
  1197)                icells(n) = icells(n) + 1
  1198)                ij = icells(n)
  1199)                indxi(ij,n) = i
  1200)                indxj(ij,n) = j
  1201)             endif               ! mm > puny

  1218)                                    ilo, ihi,       jlo, jhi,   &
  1219)                                    nghost,         ntrace,     &
  1220)                                    tracer_type,    depend,     &
  1221)                                    has_dependents, icells,     &
  1222)                                    indxi,          indxj,      &
  1223)                                    HTN,            HTE,        &
  1224)                                    hm,             xav,        &
  1225)                                    yav,            xxav,       &
  1226)                                    xyav,           yyav,       &
  1227)                                    xxxav,          xxyav,      &
  1228)                                    xyyav,          yyyav,      &
  1229)                                    dxt,            dyt,        &
  1230)                                    mm,             mc,         &
  1231)                                    mx,             my,         &
  1232)                                    mmask,                      &
  1233)                                    tm,             tc,         &
  1234)                                    tx,             ty,         &
  1235)                                    tmask)
  1236) !
  1237) ! !DESCRIPTION:
  1238) !
  1239) ! Construct fields of ice area and tracers.
  1240) !
  1241) ! !REVISION HISTORY:
  1242) !
  1243) ! authors William H. Lipscomb, LANL
  1244) !         John R. Baumgardner, LANL
  1245) !
  1246) ! !USES:
  1247) !
  1248) ! !INPUT/OUTPUT PARAMETERS:
  1249) !
  1250)       integer (kind=int_kind), intent(in) ::   &
  1251)          nx_block, ny_block  ,&! block dimensions
  1252)          ilo,ihi,jlo,jhi     ,&! beginning and end of physical domain
  1253)          nghost              ,&! number of ghost cells
  1254)          ntrace              ,&! number of tracers in use
  1255)          icells                ! number of cells with mass
  1256) 
  1257)       integer (kind=int_kind), dimension (ntrace), intent(in) ::     &
  1258)          tracer_type       ,&! = 1, 2, or 3 (see comments above)
  1259)          depend              ! tracer dependencies (see above)
  1260) 
  1261)       logical (kind=log_kind), dimension (ntrace), intent(in) ::     &
  1262)          has_dependents      ! true if a tracer has dependent tracers
  1263) 
  1264)       integer (kind=int_kind), dimension(nx_block*ny_block), intent(in) :: &
  1265)          indxi          ,&! compressed i/j indices
  1266)          indxj
  1267) 
  1268)       real (kind=dbl_kind), dimension (nx_block,ny_block),   &
  1269)          intent(in) ::   &
  1270)          hm             ,&! land/boundary mask, thickness (T-cell)
  1271)          HTN            ,&! length of northern edge of T-cell (m)
  1272)          HTE            ,&! length of eastern edge of T-cell (m)
  1273)          xav,  yav              ,&! mean T-cell values of x, y
  1274)          xxav, xyav, yyav       ,&! mean T-cell values of xx, xy, yy
  1275)          xxxav,xxyav,xyyav,yyyav,&! mean T-cell values of , xxy, xyy, yyy
  1276)          dxt            ,&! grid cell width (m)
  1277)          dyt              ! grid cell height (m)
  1278) 
  1279)       real (kind=dbl_kind), dimension (nx_block,ny_block),   &
  1280)          intent(in) ::   &
  1281)          mm            ,&! mean value of mass field
  1282)          mmask           ! = 1. if ice is present, = 0. otherwise
  1283) 
  1284)       real (kind=dbl_kind), dimension (nx_block,ny_block,max_ntrace),   &
  1285)          intent(in), optional ::   &
  1286)          tm             ,&! mean tracer
  1287)          tmask            ! = 1. if tracer is present, = 0. otherwise
  1288) 
  1289)       real (kind=dbl_kind), dimension (nx_block,ny_block),   &
  1290)          intent(out) ::   &
  1291)          mc             ,&! mass value at geometric center of cell
  1292)          mx, my           ! limited derivative of mass wrt x and y
  1293) 
  1294)       real (kind=dbl_kind), dimension (nx_block,ny_block,max_ntrace),   &
  1295)          intent(out), optional ::   &
  1296)          tc             ,&! tracer at geometric center of cell
  1297)          tx, ty           ! limited derivative of tracer wrt x and y
  1298) !
  1299) !EOP
  1300) !
  1301)       integer (kind=int_kind) ::   &
  1302)          i, j           ,&! horizontal indices
  1303)          nt, nt1        ,&! tracer indices
  1304)          ij               ! combined i/j horizontal index
  1305) 
  1306)       real (kind=dbl_kind), dimension (nx_block,ny_block) ::    &
  1307)          mxav           ,&! x coordinate of center of mass
  1308)          myav             ! y coordinate of center of mass
  1309) 
  1310)       real (kind=dbl_kind), dimension (nx_block,ny_block,max_ntrace) ::  &
  1311)          mtxav          ,&! x coordinate of center of mass*tracer
  1312)          mtyav            ! y coordinate of center of mass*tracer
  1313) 
  1314)       real (kind=dbl_kind) ::   &
  1315)          w1, w2, w3, w4, w5, w6, w7   ! work variables
  1316) 
  1317)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  1318)     ! Compute field values at the geometric center of each grid cell,
  1319)     ! and compute limited gradients in the x and y directions.
  1320)     !
  1321)     ! For second order accuracy, each state variable is approximated as
  1322)     ! a field varying linearly over x and y within each cell.  For each
  1323)     ! category, the integrated value of m(x,y) over the cell must
  1324)     ! equal mm(i,j,n)*tarea(i,j), where tarea(i,j) is the cell area.
  1325)     ! Similarly, the integrated value of m(x,y)*t(x,y) must equal
  1326)     ! the total mass*tracer, mm(i,j,n)*tm(i,j,n)*tarea(i,j).
  1327)     !
  1328)     ! These integral conditions are satisfied for linear fields if we
  1329)     ! stipulate the following:
  1330)     ! (1) The mean mass, mm, is equal to the mass at the cell centroid.
  1331)     ! (2) The mean value tm1 of type 1 tracers is equal to the value
  1332)     !     at the center of mass.
  1333)     ! (3) The mean value tm2 of type 2 tracers is equal to the value
  1334)     !     at the center of mass*tm1, where tm2 depends on tm1.
  1335)     !     (See comments at the top of the module.)
  1336)     !
  1337)     ! We want to find the value of each state variable at a standard
  1338)     ! reference point, which we choose to be the geometric center of
  1339)     ! the cell.  The geometric center is located at the intersection
  1340)     ! of the line joining the midpoints of the north and south edges
  1341)     ! with the line joining the midpoints of the east and west edges.
  1342)     ! To find the value at the geometric center, we must know the
  1343)     ! location of the cell centroid/center of mass, along with the
  1344)     ! mean value and the gradients with respect to x and y.
  1345)     !
  1346)     ! The cell gradients are first computed from the difference between
  1347)     ! values in the neighboring cells, then limited by requiring that
  1348)     ! no new extrema are created within the cell.
  1349)     !
  1350)     ! For rectangular coordinates the centroid and the geometric
  1351)     ! center coincide, which means that some of the equations in this
  1352)     ! subroutine could be simplified.  However, the full equations
  1353)     ! are retained for generality.
  1354)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  1355) 
  1356)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  1357)     ! Initialize
  1358)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  1359) 
  1360)       do j = 1, ny_block
  1361)       do i = 1, nx_block
  1362)          mc(i,j)  = c0
  1363)          mx(i,j)  = c0
  1364)          my(i,j)  = c0
  1365)          mxav(i,j) = c0
  1366)          myav(i,j) = c0
  1367)       enddo
  1368)       enddo
  1369) 
  1370)       if (present(tm)) then
  1371)          do nt = 1, ntrace
  1372)             do j = 1, ny_block
  1373)             do i = 1, nx_block
  1374)                tc(i,j,nt) = c0
  1375)                tx(i,j,nt) = c0
  1376)                ty(i,j,nt) = c0
  1377)             enddo
  1378)             enddo
  1379)          enddo
  1380)       endif
  1381)          
  1382)       ! limited gradient of mass field in each cell (except masked cells)
  1383)       ! Note: The gradient is computed in scaled coordinates with
  1384)       !       dxt = dyt = hte = htn = 1.
  1385) 
  1386)       call limited_gradient (nx_block, ny_block,   &
  1387)                              ilo, ihi, jlo, jhi,   &
  1388)                              nghost,               &
  1389)                              mm,       hm,         &
  1390)                              xav,      yav,        &
  1391)                              HTN,      HTE,        &
  1392)                              dxt,      dyt,        &
  1393)                              mx,       my)
  1394) 
  1395)       do ij = 1,icells   ! ice is present
  1396)          i = indxi(ij)
  1397)          j = indxj(ij)
  1398) 
  1399)          ! mass field at geometric center
  1400)          mc(i,j) = mm(i,j) - xav(i,j)*mx(i,j)   &
  1401)                            - yav(i,j)*my(i,j)
  1402) 
  1403)       enddo                     ! ij
  1404) 
  1405)       ! tracers
  1406) 
  1407)       if (present(tm)) then
  1408) 
  1409)        do ij = 1,icells       ! cells with mass
  1410)           i = indxi(ij)
  1411)           j = indxj(ij)
  1412) 
  1413)          ! center of mass (mxav,myav) for each cell
  1414)           mxav(i,j) = (mx(i,j)*xxav(i,j)    &
  1415)                      + my(i,j)*xyav(i,j)    &
  1416)                      + mc(i,j)*xav (i,j)) / mm(i,j)
  1417)           myav(i,j) = (mx(i,j)*xyav(i,j)    &
  1418)                      + my(i,j)*yyav(i,j)    &
  1419)                      + mc(i,j)*yav(i,j)) / mm(i,j)
  1420)        enddo
  1421) 
  1422)        do nt = 1, ntrace
  1423) 
  1424)          if (tracer_type(nt)==1) then ! independent of other tracers
  1425) 
  1426)             call limited_gradient(nx_block,     ny_block,  &
  1427)                                   ilo, ihi,     jlo, jhi,  &
  1428)                                   nghost,                  &
  1429)                                   tm(:,:,nt),   mmask,     &
  1430)                                   mxav,         myav,      &
  1431)                                   HTN,          HTE,       &
  1432)                                   dxt,          dyt,       &
  1433)                                   tx(:,:,nt),   ty(:,:,nt)) 
  1434) 
  1435)             if (has_dependents(nt)) then   ! need center of area*tracer
  1436) 
  1437)                do j = 1, ny_block
  1438)                do i = 1, nx_block
  1439)                   mtxav(i,j,nt) = c0
  1440)                   mtyav(i,j,nt) = c0
  1441)                enddo
  1442)                enddo
  1443) 
  1444) !DIR$ CONCURRENT !Cray
  1445) !cdir nodep      !NEC
  1446) !ocl novrec      !Fujitsu
  1447)                do ij = 1, icells  ! Note: no tx or ty in ghost cells
  1448)                                   ! (bound calls are later)
  1449)                   i = indxi(ij)
  1450)                   j = indxj(ij)
  1451) 
  1452)                   ! tracer value at geometric center
  1453)                   tc(i,j,nt) = tm(i,j,nt) - tx(i,j,nt)*mxav(i,j)   &
  1454)                                           - ty(i,j,nt)*myav(i,j)
  1455) 
  1456)                   if (tmask(i,j,nt) > puny) then
  1457) 
  1458)                      ! center of area*tracer
  1459)                      w1 = mc(i,j)*tc(i,j,nt)
  1460)                      w2 = mc(i,j)*tx(i,j,nt)   &
  1461)                         + mx(i,j)*tc(i,j,nt)
  1462)                      w3 = mc(i,j)*ty(i,j,nt)   &
  1463)                         + my(i,j)*tc(i,j,nt)
  1464)                      w4 = mx(i,j)*tx(i,j,nt)
  1465)                      w5 = mx(i,j)*ty(i,j,nt)   &
  1466)                         + my(i,j)*tx(i,j,nt)
  1467)                      w6 = my(i,j)*ty(i,j,nt)
  1468)                      w7 = c1 / (mm(i,j)*tm(i,j,nt))
  1469)                      mtxav(i,j,nt) = (w1*xav (i,j)  + w2*xxav (i,j)   &
  1470)                                     + w3*xyav (i,j) + w4*xxxav(i,j)   &
  1471)                                     + w5*xxyav(i,j) + w6*xyyav(i,j))   &
  1472)                                     * w7
  1473)                      mtyav(i,j,nt) = (w1*yav(i,j)   + w2*xyav (i,j)   &
  1474)                                     + w3*yyav(i,j)  + w4*xxyav(i,j)   &
  1475)                                     + w5*xyyav(i,j) + w6*yyyav(i,j))   &
  1476)                                     * w7
  1477)                   endif         ! tmask
  1478) 
  1479)                enddo            ! ij
  1480) 
  1481)             else                ! no dependents
  1482) 
  1483)                do ij = 1, icells      ! mass is present
  1484)                   i = indxi(ij)
  1485)                   j = indxj(ij)
  1486) 
  1487)                   ! tracer value at geometric center
  1488)                   tc(i,j,nt) = tm(i,j,nt) - tx(i,j,nt)*mxav(i,j)   &
  1489)                                           - ty(i,j,nt)*myav(i,j)
  1490)                enddo            ! ij
  1491) 
  1492)             endif               ! has_dependents
  1493) 
  1494)          elseif (tracer_type(nt)==2) then   ! tracer nt depends on nt1
  1495)             nt1 = depend(nt)
  1496) 
  1497)             call limited_gradient(nx_block,       ny_block,         &
  1498)                                   ilo, ihi,       jlo, jhi,         &
  1499)                                   nghost,                           &
  1500)                                   tm(:,:,nt),     tmask(:,:,nt1),   &
  1501)                                   mtxav(:,:,nt1), mtyav(:,:,nt1),   &
  1502)                                   HTN,            HTE,              &
  1503)                                   dxt,            dyt,              &
  1504)                                   tx(:,:,nt),     ty(:,:,nt))    
  1505) 
  1506)             do ij = 1, icells     ! ice is present
  1507)                i = indxi(ij)
  1508)                j = indxj(ij)
  1509)                tc(i,j,nt) = tm(i,j,nt)                    &
  1510)                           - tx(i,j,nt) * mtxav(i,j,nt1)   &
  1511)                           - ty(i,j,nt) * mtyav(i,j,nt1)
  1512)             enddo               ! ij
  1513) 
  1514)          elseif (tracer_type(nt)==3) then  ! upwind approx; gradient = 0
  1515) 
  1516)             do ij = 1, icells
  1517)                i = indxi(ij)
  1518)                j = indxj(ij)
  1519) 
  1520)                tc(i,j,nt) = tm(i,j,nt)
  1521) !               tx(i,j,nt) = c0   ! already initialized to 0.
  1522) !               ty(i,j,nt) = c0
  1523)             enddo               ! ij
  1524) 
  1525)          endif                  ! tracer_type
  1526)        enddo                    ! ntrace
  1527) 
  1528)       endif                     ! present (tm)
  1529) 
  1530)       end subroutine construct_fields

  1536) ! !IROUTINE: limited_gradient - limited gradient of a scalar field
  1537) !
  1538) ! !INTERFACE:
  1539) !
  1540)       subroutine limited_gradient (nx_block, ny_block,   &
  1541)                                    ilo, ihi, jlo, jhi,   &
  1542)                                    nghost,               &
  1543)                                    phi,      phimask,    &
  1544)                                    cnx,      cny,        &
  1545)                                    HTN,      HTE,        &
  1546)                                    dxt,      dyt,        &
  1547)                                    gx,       gy)
  1548) !
  1549) ! !DESCRIPTION:
  1550) !
  1551) ! Compute a limited gradient of the scalar field phi in scaled coordinates.
  1552) ! "Limited" means that we do not create new extrema in phi.  For
  1553) ! instance, field values at the cell corners can neither exceed the
  1554) ! maximum of phi(i,j) in the cell and its eight neighbors, nor fall
  1555) ! below the minimum.
  1556) !
  1557) ! !REVISION HISTORY:
  1558) !
  1559) ! authors William H. Lipscomb, LANL
  1560) !         John R. Baumgardner, LANL
  1561) !
  1562) ! !USES:
  1563) !
  1564) ! !INPUT/OUTPUT PARAMETERS:
  1565) !
  1566)       integer (kind=int_kind), intent(in) ::   &
  1567)           nx_block, ny_block,&! block dimensions
  1568)           ilo,ihi,jlo,jhi ,&! beginning and end of physical domain
  1569)           nghost              ! number of ghost cells
  1570) 
  1571)       real (kind=dbl_kind), dimension (nx_block,ny_block),   &
  1572)            intent (in) ::   &
  1573)           phi    ,&! input tracer field (mean values in each grid cell)
  1574)           cnx    ,&! x-coordinate of phi relative to geometric center of cell
  1575)           cny    ,&! y-coordinate of phi relative to geometric center of cell
  1576)           dxt    ,&! grid cell width (m)
  1577)           dyt    ,&! grid cell height (m)
  1578)           phimask ,&
  1579)           ! phimask(i,j) = 1 if phi(i,j) has physical meaning, = 0 otherwise.
  1580)           ! For instance, aice has no physical meaning in land cells,
  1581)           ! and hice no physical meaning where aice = 0.
  1582)           HTN    ,&! length of northern edge of T-cell (m)
  1583)           HTE      ! length of eastern edge of T-cell (m)
  1584) 
  1585)       real (kind=dbl_kind), dimension (nx_block,ny_block),   &
  1586)           intent(out) ::   &
  1587)           gx     ,&! limited x-direction gradient
  1588)           gy       ! limited y-direction gradient
  1589) !
  1590) !EOP
  1591) !
  1592)       integer (kind=int_kind) ::   &
  1593)           i, j, ij        ,&! standard indices
  1594)           icells            ! number of cells to limit
  1595) 
  1596)       integer (kind=int_kind), dimension(nx_block*ny_block) ::   &
  1597)           indxi, indxj   ! combined i/j horizontal indices
  1598) 
  1599)       real (kind=dbl_kind) ::   &
  1600)           phi_nw, phi_n, phi_ne ,&! values of phi in 8 neighbor cells
  1601)           phi_w,         phi_e  ,&
  1602)           phi_sw, phi_s, phi_se ,&
  1603)           qmn, qmx     ,&! min and max value of phi within grid cell
  1604)           pmn, pmx     ,&! min and max value of phi among neighbor cells
  1605)           w1, w2, w3, w4 ! work variables
  1606) 
  1607)       real (kind=dbl_kind) ::   &
  1608)           gxtmp, gytmp   ! temporary term for x- and y- limited gradient
  1609) 
  1610)       gx(:,:) = c0
  1611)       gy(:,:) = c0
  1612) 
  1613)       ! For nghost = 1, loop over physical cells and update ghost cells later
  1614)       ! For nghost = 2, loop over a layer of ghost cells and skip the update
  1615) 
  1616)       icells = 0
  1617)       do j = jlo-nghost+1, jhi+nghost-1
  1618)       do i = ilo-nghost+1, ihi+nghost-1
  1619)          if (phimask(i,j) > puny) then
  1620) 
  1621) !jw            icells = icells + 1
  1622) !jw            indxi(icells) = i
  1623) !jw            indxj(icells) = j
  1624) !jw         endif                  ! phimask > puny
  1625) !jw      enddo
  1626) !jw      enddo
  1627) 
  1628) !jw      do ij = 1, icells
  1629) !jw         i = indxi(ij)
  1630) !jw         j = indxj(ij)
  1631) 
  1632)          ! Store values of phi in the 8 neighbor cells.
  1633)          ! Note: phimask = 1. or 0.  If phimask = 1., use the true value;
  1634)          !  if phimask = 0., use the home cell value so that non-physical
  1635)          !  values of phi do not contribute to the gradient.
  1636)          phi_nw = phimask(i-1,j+1) * phi(i-1,j+1)   &
  1637)             + (c1-phimask(i-1,j+1))* phi(i,j)
  1638)          phi_n  = phimask(i,j+1)   * phi(i,j+1)   &
  1639)             + (c1-phimask(i,j+1))  * phi(i,j)
  1640)          phi_ne = phimask(i+1,j+1) * phi(i+1,j+1)   &
  1641)             + (c1-phimask(i+1,j+1))* phi(i,j)
  1642)          phi_w  = phimask(i-1,j)   * phi(i-1,j)   &
  1643)             + (c1-phimask(i-1,j))  * phi(i,j)
  1644)          phi_e  = phimask(i+1,j)   * phi(i+1,j)   &
  1645)             + (c1-phimask(i+1,j))  * phi(i,j)
  1646)          phi_sw = phimask(i-1,j-1) * phi(i-1,j-1)   &
  1647)             + (c1-phimask(i-1,j-1))* phi(i,j)
  1648)          phi_s  = phimask(i,j-1)   * phi(i,j-1)   &
  1649)             + (c1-phimask(i,j-1))  * phi(i,j)
  1650)          phi_se = phimask(i+1,j-1) * phi(i+1,j-1)   &
  1651)             + (c1-phimask(i+1,j-1))* phi(i,j)
  1652) 
  1653)          ! unlimited gradient components
  1654)          ! (factors of two cancel out)
  1655) 
  1656)          gxtmp = (phi_e - phi(i,j)) / (dxt(i,j)   + dxt(i+1,j))   &
  1657)                + (phi(i,j) - phi_w) / (dxt(i-1,j) + dxt(i,j)  )
  1658)          gytmp = (phi_n - phi(i,j)) / (dyt(i,j)   + dyt(i,j+1))   &
  1659)                + (phi(i,j) - phi_s) / (dyt(i,j-1) + dyt(i,j)  )
  1660) 
  1661)          ! minimum and maximum among the nine local cells
  1662)          pmn = min (phi_nw, phi_n,  phi_ne, phi_w, phi(i,j),   &
  1663)                     phi_e,  phi_sw, phi_s,  phi_se)
  1664)          pmx = max (phi_nw, phi_n,  phi_ne, phi_w, phi(i,j),   &
  1665)                     phi_e,  phi_sw, phi_s,  phi_se)
  1666) 
  1667)          pmn = pmn - phi(i,j)
  1668)          pmx = pmx - phi(i,j)
  1669) 
  1670)          ! minimum and maximum deviation of phi within the cell
  1671) 
  1672)          w1  =  (p5*HTN(i,j)   - cnx(i,j)) * gxtmp   &
  1673)               + (p5*HTE(i,j)   - cny(i,j)) * gytmp
  1674)          w2  =  (p5*HTN(i,j-1) - cnx(i,j)) * gxtmp   &
  1675)               - (p5*HTE(i,j)   + cny(i,j)) * gytmp
  1676)          w3  = -(p5*HTN(i,j-1) + cnx(i,j)) * gxtmp   &
  1677)               - (p5*HTE(i-1,j) + cny(i,j)) * gytmp
  1678)          w4  =  (p5*HTE(i-1,j) - cny(i,j)) * gytmp   &
  1679)               - (p5*HTN(i,j)   + cnx(i,j)) * gxtmp
  1680) 
  1681)          qmn = min (w1, w2, w3, w4)
  1682)          qmx = max (w1, w2, w3, w4)
  1683) 
  1684)          ! Watch for underflows here
  1685) 
  1686)          ! the limiting coefficient
  1687)          if (abs(qmn) > 1.0e-300_dbl_kind) then ! 'abs(qmn) > puny' not sufficient
  1688)             w1 = max(c0, pmn/qmn)
  1689)          else
  1690)             w1 = c1
  1691)          endif
  1692) 
  1693)          if (abs(qmx) > 1.0e-300_dbl_kind) then
  1694)             w2 = max(c0, pmx/qmx)
  1695)          else
  1696)             w2 = c1
  1697)          endif
  1698) 
  1699)          w1 = min(c1, w1, w2)
  1700) 
  1701)          ! Limit the gradient components
  1702)          gx(i,j) = w1 * gxtmp
  1703)          gy(i,j) = w1 * gytmp
  1704) 
  1705) !jw      enddo                     ! ij
  1706)           endif
  1707)        enddo
  1708)       enddo
  1709) 
  1710)       end subroutine limited_gradient

  1715) ! !IROUTINE: departure_points - compute departure points of trajectories
  1716) !
  1717) ! !INTERFACE:
  1718) !
  1719)       subroutine departure_points (nx_block,   ny_block,   &
  1720)                                    ilo, ihi,   jlo, jhi,   &
  1721)                                    nghost,     dt,   &
  1722)                                    uvel,       vvel,    &
  1723)                                    dxu,        dyu,     &
  1724)                                    HTN,        HTE,     &
  1725)                                    dpx,        dpy,     &
  1726)                                    l_dp_midpt, l_stop,   &
  1727)                                    istop,      jstop)
  1728) !
  1729) ! !DESCRIPTION:
  1730) !
  1731) ! Given velocity fields on cell corners, compute departure points
  1732) ! of back trajectories in nondimensional coordinates.
  1733) !
  1734) ! !REVISION HISTORY:
  1735) !
  1736) ! author William H. Lipscomb, LANL
  1737) !
  1738) ! !USES:
  1739) !
  1740) ! !INPUT/OUTPUT PARAMETERS:
  1741) !
  1742)       integer (kind=int_kind), intent(in) ::   &
  1743)          nx_block, ny_block,&! block dimensions
  1744)          ilo,ihi,jlo,jhi,   &! beginning and end of physical domain
  1745)          nghost              ! number of ghost cells
  1746) 
  1747)       real (kind=dbl_kind), intent(in) ::   &
  1748)          dt               ! time step (s)
  1749) 
  1750)       real (kind=dbl_kind), dimension (nx_block,ny_block), intent(in) ::   &
  1751)          uvel           ,&! x-component of velocity (m/s)
  1752)          vvel           ,&! y-component of velocity (m/s)
  1753)          dxu            ,&! E-W dimensions of U-cell (m)
  1754)          dyu            ,&! N-S dimensions of U-cell (m)
  1755)          HTN            ,&! length of north face of T-cell (m) 
  1756)          HTE              ! length of east face of T-cell (m) 
  1757) 
  1758)       real (kind=dbl_kind), dimension (nx_block,ny_block), intent(out) ::   &
  1759)          dpx           ,&! coordinates of departure points (m)
  1760)          dpy             ! coordinates of departure points (m)
  1761) 
  1762)       logical (kind=log_kind), intent(in) ::   &
  1763)          l_dp_midpt          ! if true, find departure points using
  1764)                              ! corrected midpoint velocity
  1765) 
  1766)       logical (kind=log_kind), intent(inout) ::   &
  1767)          l_stop       ! if true, abort on return
  1768) 
  1769)       integer (kind=int_kind), intent(inout) ::   &
  1770)          istop, jstop     ! indices of grid cell where model aborts 
  1771) !
  1772) !EOP
  1773) !
  1774)       integer (kind=int_kind) ::   &
  1775)          i, j, i2, j2     ! horizontal indices
  1776) 
  1777)       real (kind=dbl_kind) ::                  &
  1778)          mpx,  mpy      ,&! coordinates of midpoint of back trajectory,
  1779)                           ! relative to cell corner
  1780)          mpxt, mpyt     ,&! midpoint coordinates relative to cell center
  1781)          ump,  vmp        ! corrected velocity at midpoint
  1782) 
  1783)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  1784)     ! Estimate departure points.
  1785)     ! This estimate is 1st-order accurate in time; improve accuracy by
  1786)     !  using midpoint approximation (to add later).
  1787)     ! For nghost = 1, loop over physical cells and update ghost cells later.
  1788)     ! For nghost = 2, loop over a layer of ghost cells and skip update.
  1789)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  1790) 
  1791)       dpx(:,:) = c0
  1792)       dpy(:,:) = c0
  1793) 
  1794)       do j = jlo-nghost+1, jhi+nghost-1
  1795)       do i = ilo-nghost+1, ihi+nghost-1
  1796) 
  1797)          dpx(i,j) = -dt*uvel(i,j)
  1798)          dpy(i,j) = -dt*vvel(i,j)
  1799) 
  1800)          ! Check for values out of bounds (more than one grid cell away)
  1801)          if (dpx(i,j) < -HTN(i,j) .or. dpx(i,j) > HTN(i+1,j) .or.   &
  1802)              dpy(i,j) < -HTE(i,j) .or. dpy(i,j) > HTE(i,j+1)) then
  1803)             l_stop = .true.
  1804)             istop = i
  1805)             jstop = j
  1806)          endif
  1807) 
  1808)       enddo
  1809)       enddo
  1810) 
  1811)       if (l_stop) then
  1812)          i = istop
  1813)          j = jstop
  1814)          write (nu_diag,*) ' '
  1815)          write (nu_diag,*)   &
  1816)                     'Warning: Departure points out of bounds in remap'
  1817)          write (nu_diag,*) 'my_task, i, j =', my_task, i, j
  1818)          write (nu_diag,*) 'dpx, dpy =', dpx(i,j), dpy(i,j)
  1819)          write (nu_diag,*) 'HTN(i,j), HTN(i+1,j) =', HTN(i,j), HTN(i+1,j)
  1820)          write (nu_diag,*) 'HTE(i,j), HTE(i,j+1) =', HTE(i,j), HTE(i,j+1)
  1821)          return
  1822)       endif
  1823) 
  1824)       if (l_dp_midpt) then ! find dep pts using corrected midpt velocity 
  1825) 
  1826)        do j = jlo-nghost+1, jhi+nghost-1
  1827)        do i = ilo-nghost+1, ihi+nghost-1
  1828)          if (uvel(i,j)/=c0 .or. vvel(i,j)/=c0) then
  1829)  
  1830)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  1831)     ! Scale departure points to coordinate system in which grid cells
  1832)     ! have sides of unit length.
  1833)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  1834) 
  1835)             dpx(i,j) = dpx(i,j) / dxu(i,j)
  1836)             dpy(i,j) = dpy(i,j) / dyu(i,j)
  1837) 
  1838)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  1839)     ! Estimate midpoint of backward trajectory relative to corner (i,j).
  1840)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  1841) 
  1842)             mpx = p5 * dpx(i,j)
  1843)             mpy = p5 * dpy(i,j)
  1844)  
  1845)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  1846)     ! Determine the indices (i2,j2) of the cell where the trajectory lies.
  1847)     ! Compute the coordinates of the midpoint of the backward trajectory
  1848)     !  relative to the cell center in a stretch coordinate system
  1849)     !  with vertices at (1/2, 1/2), (1/2, -1/2), etc.
  1850)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  1851) 
  1852)             if (mpx >= c0 .and. mpy >= c0) then    ! cell (i+1,j+1)
  1853)                i2 = i+1
  1854)                j2 = j+1
  1855)                mpxt = mpx - p5
  1856)                mpyt = mpy - p5
  1857)             elseif (mpx < c0 .and. mpy < c0) then  ! cell (i,j)
  1858)                i2 = i
  1859)                j2 = j
  1860)                mpxt = mpx + p5
  1861)                mpyt = mpy + p5
  1862)             elseif (mpx >= c0 .and. mpy < c0) then ! cell (i+1,j)
  1863)                i2 = i+1
  1864)                j2 = j
  1865)                mpxt = mpx - p5
  1866)                mpyt = mpy + p5
  1867)             elseif (mpx < c0 .and. mpy >= c0) then ! cell (i,j+1)
  1868)                i2 = i
  1869)                j2 = j+1
  1870)                mpxt = mpx + p5
  1871)                mpyt = mpy - p5
  1872)             endif
  1873)             
  1874)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  1875)     ! Using a bilinear approximation, estimate the velocity at the
  1876)     ! trajectory midpoint in the (i2,j2) reference frame.
  1877)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  1878)  
  1879)             ump = uvel(i2-1,j2-1)*(mpxt-p5)*(mpyt-p5)     &
  1880)                 - uvel(i2,  j2-1)*(mpxt+p5)*(mpyt-p5)     &
  1881)                 + uvel(i2,  j2  )*(mpxt+p5)*(mpyt+p5)     &  
  1882)                 - uvel(i2-1,j2  )*(mpxt-p5)*(mpyt+p5)
  1883)  
  1884)             vmp = vvel(i2-1,j2-1)*(mpxt-p5)*(mpyt-p5)     &
  1885)                 - vvel(i2,  j2-1)*(mpxt+p5)*(mpyt-p5)     &
  1886)                 + vvel(i2,  j2  )*(mpxt+p5)*(mpyt+p5)     &
  1887)                 - vvel(i2-1,j2  )*(mpxt-p5)*(mpyt+p5)
  1888)  
  1889)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  1890)     ! Use the midpoint velocity to estimate the coordinates of the
  1891)     !  departure point relative to corner (i,j).
  1892)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  1893)  
  1894)             dpx(i,j) = -dt * ump
  1895)             dpy(i,j) = -dt * vmp
  1896)  
  1897)          endif               ! nonzero velocity
  1898) 
  1899)        enddo                 ! i
  1900)        enddo                 ! j
  1901)  
  1902)       endif                  ! l_dp_midpt
  1903) 
  1904)       end subroutine departure_points

  1912) ! !INTERFACE:
  1913) !
  1914)       subroutine locate_triangles (nx_block,     ny_block,   &
  1915)                                    ilo, ihi,     jlo, jhi,   &
  1916)                                    nghost,       edge,       &
  1917)                                    icells,                   &
  1918)                                    indxi,        indxj,      &
  1919)                                    dpx,          dpy,        &
  1920)                                    dxu,          dyu,        &
  1921)                                    xp,           yp,         &
  1922)                                    iflux,        jflux,      &
  1923)                                    triarea,                  &
  1924)                                    l_fixed_area, edgearea)
  1925) !
  1926) 
  1927) ! !DESCRIPTION:
  1928) !
  1929) ! Compute areas and vertices of transport triangles for north or

  1999)          indxid         ,&! compressed index in i-direction
  2000)          indxjd           ! compressed index in j-direction
  2001) 
  2002)       real (kind=dbl_kind), dimension(nx_block,ny_block) ::   &
  2003)          dx, dy         ,&! scaled departure points

  2006)          areafac_r        ! area scale factor at right corner
  2007) 
  2008)       real (kind=dbl_kind) ::   &
  2009)          xcl, ycl       ,&! coordinates of left corner point
  2010)                           ! (relative to midpoint of edge)
  2011)          xdl, ydl       ,&! left departure point
  2012)          xil, yil       ,&! left intersection point
  2013)          xcr, ycr       ,&! right corner point
  2014)          xdr, ydr       ,&! right departure point
  2015)          xir, yir       ,&! right intersection point
  2016)          xic, yic       ,&! x-axis intersection point
  2017)          xicl, yicl     ,&! left-hand x-axis intersection point
  2018)          xicr, yicr     ,&! right-hand x-axis intersection point
  2019)          xdm, ydm       ,&! midpoint of segment connecting DL and DR;
  2020)                           ! shifted if l_fixed_area = T
  2021)          dxc            ,&! xcr - xcl
  2022)          dxd            ,&! xdr - xdl
  2023)          md             ,&! slope of line connecting DL and DR
  2024)          mdl            ,&! slope of line connecting DL and DM

  2026)          ishift_tl, jshift_tl ,&! i,j indices of TL cell relative to edge
  2027)          ishift_bl, jshift_bl ,&! i,j indices of BL cell relative to edge
  2028)          ishift_tr, jshift_tr ,&! i,j indices of TR cell relative to edge
  2029)          ishift_br, jshift_br ,&! i,j indices of BR cell relative to edge
  2030)          ishift_tc, jshift_tc ,&! i,j indices of TC cell relative to edge
  2031)          ishift_bc, jshift_bc ,&! i,j indices of BC cell relative to edge

  2033)          area3, area4         ,&!

  2038)          areafact         ! = 1 for positive flux, -1 for negative
  2039) 
  2040)       real (kind=dbl_kind), dimension(nx_block,ny_block) ::   &
  2041)          areasum          ! sum of triangle areas for a given edge

  2043)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  2044)     ! Triangle notation:
  2045)     ! For each edge, there are 20 triangles that can contribute,
  2046)     ! but many of these are mutually exclusive.  It turns out that
  2047)     ! at most 5 triangles can contribute to transport integrals at once.
  2048)     !
  2049)     ! See Figure 3 in DB for pictures of these triangles.
  2050)     ! See Table 1 in DB for logical conditions.
  2051)     !
  2052)     ! For the north edge, DB refer to these triangles as:
  2053)     ! (1) NW, NW1, W, W2
  2054)     ! (2) NE, NE1, E, E2
  2055)     ! (3) NW2, W1, NE2, E1
  2056)     ! (4) H1a, H1b, N1a, N1b
  2057)     ! (5) H2a, H2b, N2a, N2b
  2058)     !
  2059)     ! For the east edge, DB refer to these triangles as:
  2060)     ! (1) NE, NE1, N, N2
  2061)     ! (2) SE, SE1, S, S2
  2062)     ! (3) NE2, N1, SE2, S1
  2063)     ! (4) H1a, H1b, E1a, E2b
  2064)     ! (5) H2a, H2b, E2a, E2b
  2065)     !
  2066)     ! The code below works for either north or east edges.
  2067)     ! The respective triangle labels are:
  2068)     ! (1) TL,  TL1, BL,  BL2
  2069)     ! (2) TR,  TR1, BR,  BR2
  2070)     ! (3) TL2, BL1, TR2, BR1
  2071)     ! (4) BC1a, BC1b, TC1a, TC2b
  2072)     ! (5) BC2a, BC2b, TC2a, TC2b
  2073)     ! 
  2074)     ! where the cell labels are:
  2075)     ! 
  2076)     !          |        |
  2077)     !     TL   |   TC   |   TR     (top left, center, right)
  2078)     !          |        |
  2079)     !   ------------------------
  2080)     !          |        |
  2081)     !     BL   |   BC   |   BR     (bottom left, center, right)
  2082)     !          |        |
  2083)     !
  2084)     ! and the transport is across the edge between cells TC and TB.
  2085)     !
  2086)     ! Departure points are scaled to a local coordinate system
  2087)     !  whose origin is at the midpoint of the edge.
  2088)     ! In this coordinate system, the lefthand corner CL = (-0.5,0)
  2089)     !  and the righthand corner CR = (0.5, 0).
  2090)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  2091)   
  2092)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  2093)     ! Initialize
  2094)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  2095) 
  2096)       areafac_c(:,:) = c0
  2097)       areafac_l(:,:) = c0
  2098)       areafac_r(:,:) = c0
  2099)       do ng = 1, ngroups
  2100)          do j = 1, ny_block
  2101)          do i = 1, nx_block
  2102)             triarea (i,j,ng) = c0
  2103)             areafact(i,j,ng) = c0
  2104)             iflux   (i,j,ng) = i
  2105)             jflux   (i,j,ng) = j
  2106)          enddo
  2107)          enddo
  2108)          do nv = 0, nvert
  2109)             do j = 1, ny_block
  2110)             do i = 1, nx_block
  2111)                xp(i,j,nv,ng) = c0
  2112)                yp(i,j,nv,ng) = c0
  2113)             enddo
  2114)             enddo
  2115)          enddo
  2116)       enddo
  2117) 
  2118)       if (trim(edge) == 'north') then
  2119) 
  2120)          ! loop size
  2121) 
  2122)          ib = ilo
  2123)          ie = ihi 
  2124)          jb = jlo - nghost            ! lowest j index is a ghost cell
  2125)          je = jhi
  2126) 
  2127)          ! index shifts for neighbor cells
  2128) 
  2129)          ishift_tl = -1
  2130)          jshift_tl =  1
  2131)          ishift_bl = -1
  2132)          jshift_bl =  0
  2133)          ishift_tr =  1
  2134)          jshift_tr =  1
  2135)          ishift_br =  1
  2136)          jshift_br =  0
  2137)          ishift_tc =  0
  2138)          jshift_tc =  1
  2139)          ishift_bc =  0
  2140)          jshift_bc =  0
  2141) 
  2142)          ! area scale factor
  2143) 
  2144)          do j = jb, je
  2145)          do i = ib, ie
  2146)             areafac_l(i,j) = dxu(i-1,j)*dyu(i-1,j) 
  2147)             areafac_r(i,j) = dxu(i,j)*dyu(i,j) 
  2148)             areafac_c(i,j) = p5*(areafac_l(i,j) + areafac_r(i,j))
  2149)          enddo
  2150)          enddo
  2151) 
  2152)       else                      ! east edge
  2153) 
  2154)          ! loop size
  2155) 
  2156)          ib = ilo - nghost            ! lowest i index is a ghost cell
  2157)          ie = ihi
  2158)          jb = jlo
  2159)          je = jhi
  2160) 
  2161)          ! index shifts for neighbor cells
  2162) 
  2163)          ishift_tl =  1
  2164)          jshift_tl =  1
  2165)          ishift_bl =  0
  2166)          jshift_bl =  1
  2167)          ishift_tr =  1
  2168)          jshift_tr = -1
  2169)          ishift_br =  0
  2170)          jshift_br = -1
  2171)          ishift_tc =  1
  2172)          jshift_tc =  0
  2173)          ishift_bc =  0
  2174)          jshift_bc =  0
  2175) 
  2176)          ! area scale factors
  2177) 
  2178)          do j = jb, je
  2179)          do i = ib, ie
  2180)             areafac_l(i,j) = dxu(i,j)*dyu(i,j) 
  2181)             areafac_r(i,j) = dxu(i,j-1)*dyu(i,j-1)
  2182)             areafac_c(i,j) = p5 * (areafac_l(i,j) + areafac_r(i,j))
  2183)          enddo
  2184)          enddo
  2185) 
  2186)       endif
  2187) 
  2188)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  2189)     ! Compute mask for edges with nonzero departure areas
  2190)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  2191) 
  2192)       if (l_fixed_area) then
  2193)          icellsd = 0
  2194)          do j = jb, je
  2195)          do i = ib, ie
  2196)             if (edgearea(i,j) /= c0) then
  2197)                icellsd = icellsd + 1
  2198)                indxid(icellsd) = i
  2199)                indxjd(icellsd) = j
  2200)             endif
  2201)          enddo
  2202)          enddo
  2203)       else
  2204)          icellsd = 0
  2205)          if (trim(edge) == 'north') then
  2206)             do j = jb, je
  2207)             do i = ib, ie
  2208)                if (dpx(i-1,j)/=c0 .or. dpy(i-1,j)/=c0   &
  2209)                                   .or.                  &
  2210)                      dpx(i,j)/=c0 .or.   dpy(i,j)/=c0) then
  2211)                   icellsd = icellsd + 1
  2212)                   indxid(icellsd) = i
  2213)                   indxjd(icellsd) = j
  2214)                endif
  2215)             enddo
  2216)             enddo
  2217)          else       ! east edge
  2218)             do j = jb, je
  2219)             do i = ib, ie
  2220)                if (dpx(i,j-1)/=c0 .or. dpy(i,j-1)/=c0   &
  2221)                                   .or.                  &
  2222)                      dpx(i,j)/=c0 .or.   dpy(i,j)/=c0) then
  2223)                   icellsd = icellsd + 1
  2224)                   indxid(icellsd) = i
  2225)                   indxjd(icellsd) = j
  2226)                endif
  2227)             enddo
  2228)             enddo
  2229)          endif       ! edge = north/east
  2230)       endif          ! l_fixed_area
  2231) 
  2232)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  2233)     ! Scale the departure points
  2234)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  2235) 
  2236)       do j = 1, je
  2237)       do i = 1, ie
  2238)          dx(i,j) = dpx(i,j) / dxu(i,j)
  2239)          dy(i,j) = dpy(i,j) / dyu(i,j)
  2240)       enddo
  2241)       enddo
  2242) 
  2243)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  2244)     ! Compute departure regions, divide into triangles, and locate
  2245)     !  vertices of each triangle.
  2246)     ! Work in a nondimensional coordinate system in which lengths are
  2247)     !  scaled by the local metric coefficients (dxu and dyu).
  2248)     ! Note: The do loop includes north faces of the j = 1 ghost cells
  2249)     !       when edge = 'north'.  The loop includes east faces of i = 1
  2250)     !       ghost cells when edge = 'east'.
  2251)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  2252) 
  2253)       do ij = 1, icellsd
  2254)          i = indxid(ij)
  2255)          j = indxjd(ij)
  2256)   
  2257)          xcl = -p5
  2258)          ycl =  c0
  2259) 
  2260)          xcr =  p5
  2261)          ycr =  c0
  2262) 
  2263)          ! Departure points
  2264) 
  2265)          if (trim(edge) == 'north') then ! north edge
  2266)             xdl = xcl + dx(i-1,j)
  2267)             ydl = ycl + dy(i-1,j)
  2268)             xdr = xcr + dx(i,j)
  2269)             ydr = ycr + dy(i,j)
  2270)          else                   ! east edge; rotate trajectory by pi/2
  2271)             xdl = xcl - dy(i,j)
  2272)             ydl = ycl + dx(i,j)
  2273)             xdr = xcr - dy(i,j-1)
  2274)             ydr = ycr + dx(i,j-1)
  2275)          endif
  2276) 
  2277)          xdm = p5 * (xdr + xdl)
  2278)          ydm = p5 * (ydr + ydl)
  2279) 
  2280)          ! Intersection points
  2281) 
  2282)          xil = xcl
  2283)          yil = (xcl*(ydm-ydl) + xdm*ydl - xdl*ydm) / (xdm - xdl)
  2284)          
  2285)          xir = xcr
  2286)          yir = (xcr*(ydr-ydm) - xdm*ydr + xdr*ydm) / (xdr - xdm) 
  2287)          
  2288)          md = (ydr - ydl) / (xdr - xdl)
  2289)          
  2290)          if (abs(md) > puny) then
  2291)             xic = xdl - ydl/md
  2292)          else
  2293)             xic = c0
  2294)          endif
  2295)          yic = c0
  2296) 
  2297)          xicl = xic
  2298)          yicl = yic
  2299)          xicr = xic
  2300)          yicr = yic
  2301) 
  2302)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  2303)     ! Locate triangles in TL cell (NW for north edge, NE for east edge)
  2304)     ! and BL cell (W for north edge, N for east edge).
  2305)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  2306) 
  2307)          if (yil > c0 .and. xdl < xcl .and. ydl >= c0) then
  2308) 
  2309)          ! TL (group 1)
  2310) 
  2311)             ng = 1
  2312)             xp    (i,j,1,ng) = xcl
  2313)             yp    (i,j,1,ng) = ycl
  2314)             xp    (i,j,2,ng) = xil
  2315)             yp    (i,j,2,ng) = yil
  2316)             xp    (i,j,3,ng) = xdl
  2317)             yp    (i,j,3,ng) = ydl
  2318)             iflux   (i,j,ng) = i + ishift_tl
  2319)             jflux   (i,j,ng) = j + jshift_tl
  2320)             areafact(i,j,ng) = -areafac_l(i,j)
  2321) 
  2322)          elseif (yil < c0 .and. xdl < xcl .and. ydl < c0) then
  2323) 
  2324)          ! BL (group 1)
  2325) 
  2326)             ng = 1
  2327)             xp    (i,j,1,ng) = xcl
  2328)             yp    (i,j,1,ng) = ycl
  2329)             xp    (i,j,2,ng) = xdl
  2330)             yp    (i,j,2,ng) = ydl
  2331)             xp    (i,j,3,ng) = xil
  2332)             yp    (i,j,3,ng) = yil
  2333)             iflux   (i,j,ng) = i + ishift_bl
  2334)             jflux   (i,j,ng) = j + jshift_bl
  2335)             areafact(i,j,ng) = areafac_l(i,j)
  2336) 
  2337)          elseif (yil < c0 .and. xdl < xcl .and. ydl >= c0) then
  2338) 
  2339)          ! TL1 (group 1)
  2340) 
  2341)             ng = 1
  2342)             xp    (i,j,1,ng) = xcl
  2343)             yp    (i,j,1,ng) = ycl
  2344)             xp    (i,j,2,ng) = xdl
  2345)             yp    (i,j,2,ng) = ydl
  2346)             xp    (i,j,3,ng) = xic
  2347)             yp    (i,j,3,ng) = yic
  2348)             iflux   (i,j,ng) = i + ishift_tl
  2349)             jflux   (i,j,ng) = j + jshift_tl
  2350)             areafact(i,j,ng) = areafac_l(i,j)
  2351) 
  2352)          ! BL1 (group 3)
  2353) 
  2354)             ng = 3
  2355)             xp    (i,j,1,ng) = xcl
  2356)             yp    (i,j,1,ng) = ycl
  2357)             xp    (i,j,2,ng) = xic
  2358)             yp    (i,j,2,ng) = yic
  2359)             xp    (i,j,3,ng) = xil
  2360)             yp    (i,j,3,ng) = yil
  2361)             iflux   (i,j,ng) = i + ishift_bl
  2362)             jflux   (i,j,ng) = j + jshift_bl
  2363)             areafact(i,j,ng) = areafac_l(i,j)
  2364) 
  2365)          elseif (yil > c0 .and. xdl < xcl .and. ydl < c0) then
  2366) 
  2367)          ! TL2 (group 3)
  2368) 
  2369)             ng = 3
  2370)             xp    (i,j,1,ng) = xcl
  2371)             yp    (i,j,1,ng) = ycl
  2372)             xp    (i,j,2,ng) = xil
  2373)             yp    (i,j,2,ng) = yil
  2374)             xp    (i,j,3,ng) = xic
  2375)             yp    (i,j,3,ng) = yic
  2376)             iflux   (i,j,ng) = i + ishift_tl
  2377)             jflux   (i,j,ng) = j + jshift_tl
  2378)             areafact(i,j,ng) = -areafac_l(i,j)
  2379) 
  2380)          ! BL2 (group 1)
  2381) 
  2382)             ng = 1
  2383)             xp    (i,j,1,ng) = xcl
  2384)             yp    (i,j,1,ng) = ycl
  2385)             xp    (i,j,2,ng) = xic
  2386)             yp    (i,j,2,ng) = yic
  2387)             xp    (i,j,3,ng) = xdl
  2388)             yp    (i,j,3,ng) = ydl
  2389)             iflux   (i,j,ng) = i + ishift_bl
  2390)             jflux   (i,j,ng) = j + jshift_bl
  2391)             areafact(i,j,ng) = -areafac_l(i,j)
  2392) 
  2393)          endif                  ! TL and BL triangles
  2394) 
  2395)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  2396)     ! Locate triangles in TR cell (NE for north edge, SE for east edge)
  2397)     ! and in BR cell (E for north edge, S for east edge).
  2398)     !-------------------------------------------------------------------
  2399) 
  2400)          if (yir > c0 .and. xdr >= xcr .and. ydr >= c0) then
  2401) 
  2402)          ! TR (group 2)
  2403) 
  2404)             ng = 2
  2405)             xp    (i,j,1,ng) = xcr
  2406)             yp    (i,j,1,ng) = ycr
  2407)             xp    (i,j,2,ng) = xdr
  2408)             yp    (i,j,2,ng) = ydr
  2409)             xp    (i,j,3,ng) = xir
  2410)             yp    (i,j,3,ng) = yir
  2411)             iflux   (i,j,ng) = i + ishift_tr
  2412)             jflux   (i,j,ng) = j + jshift_tr
  2413)             areafact(i,j,ng) = -areafac_r(i,j)
  2414) 
  2415)          elseif (yir < c0 .and. xdr >= xcr .and. ydr < c0) then
  2416) 
  2417)          ! BR (group 2)
  2418) 
  2419)             ng = 2
  2420)             xp    (i,j,1,ng) = xcr
  2421)             yp    (i,j,1,ng) = ycr
  2422)             xp    (i,j,2,ng) = xir
  2423)             yp    (i,j,2,ng) = yir
  2424)             xp    (i,j,3,ng) = xdr
  2425)             yp    (i,j,3,ng) = ydr
  2426)             iflux   (i,j,ng) = i + ishift_br
  2427)             jflux   (i,j,ng) = j + jshift_br
  2428)             areafact(i,j,ng) = areafac_r(i,j)
  2429) 
  2430)          elseif (yir < c0 .and. xdr >= xcr  .and. ydr >= c0) then 
  2431) 
  2432)          ! TR1 (group 2)
  2433) 
  2434)             ng = 2
  2435)             xp    (i,j,1,ng) = xcr
  2436)             yp    (i,j,1,ng) = ycr

  3311) ! !DESCRIPTION:
  3312) !
  3313) ! For each triangle, find the coordinates of the quadrature points needed
  3314) !  to compute integrals of linear, quadratic, or cubic polynomials,
  3315) !  using formulas from A.H. Stroud, Approximate Calculation of Multiple
  3316) !  Integrals, Prentice-Hall, 1971.  (Section 8.8, formula 3.1.)
  3317) ! Linear functions can be integrated exactly by evaluating the function 
  3318) !  at just one point (the midpoint).  Quadratic functions require
  3319) !  3 points, and cubics require 4 points.
  3320) ! The default is cubic, but the code can be sped up slightly using 
  3321) !  linear or quadratic integrals, usually with little loss of accuracy.
  3322) !
  3323) ! The formulas are as follows:
  3324) !
  3325) ! I1 = integral of f(x,y)*dA
  3326) !    = A * f(x0,y0)
  3327) ! where A is the traingle area and (x0,y0) is the midpoint.
  3328) !
  3329) ! I2 = A * (f(x1,y1) + f(x2,y2) + f(x3,y3))
  3330) ! where these three points are located halfway between the midpoint
  3331) ! and the three vertics of the triangle.
  3332) !
  3333) ! I3 = A * [ -9/16 *  f(x0,y0)
  3334) !           + 25/48 * (f(x1,y1) + f(x2,y2) + f(x3,y3))]
  3335) ! where (x0,y0) is the midpoint, and the other three points are
  3336) ! located 2/5 of the way from the midpoint to the three vertices.
  3337) !

  3521)            dimension (nx_block, ny_block) ::   &
  3522)            mflx

  3535)            i, j, ij      ,&! horizontal indices of edge
  3536)            i2, j2        ,&! horizontal indices of cell contributing transport
